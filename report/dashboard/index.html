Who funds Nepal, and how much actually reaches it

Every year, other countries and big organisations send Nepal billions of dollars in aid. This page shows who gives it, how much really arrives, and where every number comes from, so you can check it yourself. New here? Start with the short guide below.

How to read this dashboard

Aid to Nepal is reported by two sides that count differently. This dashboard shows both and explains the difference in plain terms.

The big picture

Who says how much: the two sides vs an independent check

The blue line is what donors report giving. The gray line is an independent World Bank check (it sits almost exactly on the blue line, so the donor side looks reliable). The red line is what Nepal says it received — higher in some years because it counts loans in full and includes China, India and Gulf donors.

Amounts in US dollars, millions. Recipient side follows Nepal's fiscal year.
Who funds Nepal

Biggest donors, each side

Both sides agree on the ranking: the World Bank and the Asian Development Bank are the largest, then Japan, the United States, the United Kingdom and the EU. India shows up only on Nepal's side, because India does not report to the international system.

Tip: click a bar — or a chip under the chart — to open that funder's profile. On Nepal's side only funders with a matching profile open (India, China, Japan, the UK, Germany, Switzerland, Norway).

A closer look

China: big promises, smaller delivery

The large Chinese figures for Nepal are mostly promises (commitments). The money Nepal records actually arriving is much smaller and fell after 2018. The international aid statistics do not track China at all.

Promises: AidData (to 2021). Delivered: Nepal's own report.

The recipient's own books

Projects on the Government of Nepal's books (DFIMS, MoF)

Nepal's Ministry of Finance publishes its own project-by-project ledger, DFIMS. Our copy of all 2,925 rows sums to the API's published totals to the dollar. These are the recipient side's own records — not automatically consistent with donor reporting.
