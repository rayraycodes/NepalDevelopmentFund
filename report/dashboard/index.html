What Nepal receives, and what donors report giving — compared, not merged.

A verifiable, fully sourced view of official development finance to Nepal. Every figure is traced to a primary source, separated into commitments and disbursements, and tagged by confidence. The two ledgers are compared rather than added.

The triangulation

Donor side vs recipient side vs anchor

The OECD donor-side total and the World Bank net-ODA anchor overlap almost to the dollar (both OECD-rooted). Nepal's own Development Cooperation Report runs higher in 2016–2019 and peaks near US$2.0bn in 2019. The divergence is real and explained below.

US$ millions, current. Donor = OECD DAC2A (net); recipient = Nepal DCR (gross, by fiscal-year start); anchor = World Bank DT.ODA.ODAT.CD.
Who funds Nepal

Top donors, by ledger side

The ranking agrees across both ledgers: the World Bank and ADB lead, then Japan, the US, the UK and the EU. India appears only on the recipient side (it is a non-DAC donor); ADB is larger there because Nepal records gross loan disbursement.

A closer look

China: large commitments, modest recorded disbursements

China's headline numbers for Nepal are commitments and pledges. The disbursements Nepal actually records (in its own Development Cooperation Report) are small and fell sharply after 2018. OECD statistics do not capture China at all.

Commitments: AidData GCDF v3.0 (to 2021). Disbursements: Nepal DCR.

Data-integrity note. Commitments and disbursements are never added together. The donor-side headline is OECD DAC2A only; the recipient-side headline is the Nepal DCR only; the two are compared, never merged. IATI is de-duplicated by identifier. China, India and the Gulf funds are absent from OECD and come from Nepal's own reporting. Nothing is fabricated: unverifiable figures are left blank and labelled MISSING.
